# AI Use by Office Employees: Threats and Defensive Responses

**Author:** Yuval Sinay  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Year:** 2026

## Purpose

This tool helps organizations identify risks created by office employees using generative AI, copilots, chatbots, AI-enabled productivity applications, browser extensions, and connected AI agents. It links common threat scenarios to practical preventive, detective, and corrective controls.

## Included Files

- [`AI-Office-Use-Risk-Assessment.md`](./AI-Office-Use-Risk-Assessment.md): Core assessment method and control catalogue.
- [`assessment-template.csv`](./assessment-template.csv): Editable worksheet for recording organizational findings.

## Recommended Use

1. Define the business units, employee groups, AI services, and data types in scope.
2. Review each threat scenario with security, privacy, legal, IT, and business representatives.
3. Record likelihood, impact, existing controls, evidence, gaps, and planned actions.
4. Assign an accountable owner and target date for every material gap.
5. Reassess after major AI deployments, policy changes, incidents, or changes in supplier terms.

## Important Note

Risk scores support prioritization but do not replace professional judgment. A lower numerical score should not override legal, regulatory, safety, national-security, or mission-critical considerations.
