# AI Security Tooling

A collection of practical tools for assessing, governing, and securing organizational use of artificial intelligence.

**Author:** Yuval Sinay  
**Year:** 2026

## Repository Structure

Each tool is maintained in its own folder. Every folder should include:

- A tool-specific `README.md`
- The assessment, checklist, framework, or implementation artifact
- Supporting templates or examples
- Version and ownership information

## Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Status |
|---|---|---|
| [AI Use by Office Employees: Threats and Defensive Responses](./AI-Use-by-Office-Employees-Threats-and-Defensive-Responses/) | Identify common risks arising from employee use of AI tools and define proportionate defensive responses. | Initial release |

## Intended Audience

- Chief information security officers
- Security architects
- AI governance and risk teams
- Privacy and legal teams
- Internal audit teams
- Business and technology managers

## Use

These tools are designed to support organizational assessment and decision-making. They do not replace legal advice, regulatory analysis, product-specific testing, or a formal risk assessment.
